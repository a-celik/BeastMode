Background and Motivation

Social media and entertainment are such pervasive parts of millennials' lives. We want to study the intersection of these two. Is it possible to predict box office success of movies through sentiments expressed on social media? Stay tuned for more!

Abstract

Our overall scientific goal is to be able to do sentiment analysis on text, and see if we are able to predict box office successes from our analysis.

In general, we will use text-sentiment analysis to measure the positivity of the online reviews for each film in our data set. Afterwards, we will use these results to predict their success in the box office, as measured by opening gross income, the amount of money a movie makes in its opening weekend.

If our analysis proves to be correct, our findings can help production companies predict the performance of their film from the responses of critics at an early stage. Likewise, if these firms can measure the audiences’ responses to trailers/teasers, they can use this knowledge to alter their marketing efforts and achieve better financial results. Moreover, theaters can also potentially use this analysis to determine when they should pull a movie from cinemas.
